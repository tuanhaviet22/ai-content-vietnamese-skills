# Vietnamese SEO Guidelines

## Market Principles

- Vietnamese SEO content should usually support both search visibility and trust-building.
- Many buyers compare informally before converting: Google search, Facebook groups, TikTok, Shopee, friends, and direct messages.
- Content should reduce perceived risk: price anxiety, fear of complexity, fear of wasting money, fear of choosing the wrong vendor.
- Local examples are often more persuasive than generic global examples.

## Writing Style

- Use clear Vietnamese, short paragraphs, and practical wording.
- Avoid overusing exact-match keywords.
- Avoid translated English phrases that sound unnatural.
- Explain concepts with familiar Vietnamese business situations.
- Prefer direct usefulness over broad motivational claims.

## Trust Signals

Use trust signals where relevant:

- specific examples from Vietnamese businesses
- checklists and decision criteria
- transparent limitations
- pricing or cost considerations
- before and after workflow examples
- common mistakes
- FAQs that answer real objections
- soft CTA tied to the reader's next step

## Search Intent Patterns

Informational intent:

- Explain the concept clearly.
- Add examples, checklist, mistakes, and next steps.
- Use soft CTA.

Commercial investigation intent:

- Include comparison criteria.
- Explain who each option is for.
- Address price, setup effort, and risk.
- Use product CTA after the reader understands the decision.

Transactional intent:

- Make offer, benefits, proof, pricing, and CTA easy to find.
- Reduce friction.
- Add FAQ and trust signals.

Branding intent:

- Build authority through insight, original perspective, and proof.
- Use a lighter CTA.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Writing only for traffic with no conversion path.
- Listing features without connecting them to business pain.
- Using generic advice that could apply to any country.
- Pushing CTA too early for informational queries.
- Making the product sound perfect for everyone.
- Ignoring objections around cost, time, complexity, and trust.
