# SERP Analysis Framework

Use this framework when analyzing provided SERP data or inferring likely SERP patterns.

## Extract From Each Result

- URL or source name
- title angle
- content type
- target audience
- H1 and major H2 sections
- depth: thin, medium, comprehensive
- conversion mechanism: CTA, lead form, demo, free tool, consultation, product signup
- trust signals: author, case study, data, reviews, examples, pricing, screenshots
- freshness: visible year, recent update, trend-based language

## Identify SERP-Level Pattern

Look for:

- repeated definitions
- repeated checklists
- repeated comparison tables
- repeated "top tools" format
- repeated beginner guide structure
- repeated pricing or buying intent sections
- repeated FAQs

If 3 or more top pages share a section, treat it as a likely must-have.

## Classify Content Type

- Blog guide: teaches and explains.
- Listicle: ranks or compares options.
- Landing page: sells one offer.
- Comparison page: compares two or more options.
- Tool page: solves the task directly.
- Category page: aggregates products or services.
- Local service page: targets location plus service.

## Determine Search Intent

Use the dominant page types:

- Mostly guides: informational.
- Mostly lists/comparisons: commercial investigation.
- Mostly product or service pages: transactional.
- Mostly brand pages: navigational.
- Mixed SERP: split intent; satisfy the dominant intent first and add support sections for secondary intent.

## Find Content Gaps

Look for missing:

- Vietnam-specific examples
- practical workflow
- pricing clarity
- beginner explanation
- buyer objections
- comparison criteria
- examples from small businesses
- implementation steps
- mistakes and risks
- templates or checklists

## SERP Risk Labels

Low risk:

- SERP is weak, thin, outdated, or generic.

Medium risk:

- SERP has decent guides but clear gaps or poor local relevance.

High risk:

- SERP is dominated by strong brands, tools, marketplaces, or high-authority comparison sites.

Very high risk:

- SERP is dominated by government, news, medical, finance, or strong domain-authority results where E-E-A-T is critical.
